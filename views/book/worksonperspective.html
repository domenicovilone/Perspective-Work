For several months now, the world has suffered many drastic changes as a result of Covid-19. This pandemic has led governments to carry out new rules and regulations such as those of confinement where populations in most countries have been ordered to remain inside their homes as a means of social isolation in hopes of diminishing the spread of the Coronavirus. As people discuss the benefits and drawbacks of staying at home, a verdict remains to be delivered on whether online education and telecommuting can become the new normal in the future. From implementing social distancing regulations, to encouraging strict health measures in public and private gatherings, governments have played a dominant role in dealing with this virus.

In a world where creating and spreading information on digital platforms has become easier than ever, conspiracy theories and alternative facts have both spread through social media platforms, thereby resulting in a distortion of the information provided by mainstream media. Skepticism on the origin of the virus or its very own existence has grown to be consequential. Nevertheless, whether the published information was correct or erroneous, media outlets and social media platforms have taken on a defining role in informing people and providing context on the pandemic.

This book explores these aforementioned issues by not only telling the story from a single person’s perspective and observations, but from that of 28 different individuals varying in age, most of whom reside in Montreal, while others reached out from surrounding cities such as Ottawa and Trois-Rivière. In short, these participants were asked to give an honest reply to a set of six questions relating to the pandemic, in which they would share their own personal experiences. Finally, all answers were grouped together for this book in order to provide a multi-dimensional view of the Covid-19 pandemic in this particular region of the world.

While reading this book, you will be exposed to a variety of positives and negatives of quarantine, as well as how it reshaped people’s lifestyles. Not to mention, you will familiarize yourself with different states of mind, views on the media’s coverage of the pandemic, experiences within the workplace, as well as habits and activities people have picked up or lost during quarantine.

In honor of the first issue of the series Works On Perspective, our team hopes that you take away a thing or two from this reading.

Personally speaking, what would you say have been the positives and negatives of quarantine thus far? Moreover, would you say the positives outweigh the negatives somehow? Or perhaps the other way around?

Answer 01

On a positive note, reassessing. As a man in my 20’s, I do not exactly have my life figured out yet. Just as many others do not. I have tried to maintain an image of myself to appear as though I belong to a certain status, but I now understand that the process of development takes time and that it is okay to be lost or not appeal to any statuses.


The pandemic taught me to take my time with myself and to strictly focus on the person I would like to become. Not my image. There is a lot of work that needs to get done, and so right now is the perfect time to lay out any plans and adapt to a healthier lifestyle.


Sadly, some of us are not fortunate enough to be surrounded by peace in order to clearly deal with our life goals. In my case, I deal with my battles one day at a time and I become stronger through suffering. No one is free from that. I would say that the positives outweigh the negatives as long as I do not let circumstances define me.

Answer 02

In terms of the positives, families have grown closer together since lockdown has left them with no choice but to remain inside their homes while trying to figure out the different activities they could entertain themselves with.


As for the negatives, people with friends or family members living elsewhere, such as grandparents residing in retirement homes, have refrained from meeting up with them as per the government's social distancing regulations. In consequence, this has caused a divide between many people.

Answer 03

Positively speaking, I suppose it has given us all a new way of viewing life as a whole. The “old normal” as some would like to call it, was something many of us took for granted.


However, with that being said, this new perspective has altered our thought process in terms of how we go about doing things. For instance, we have learned to become more aware and cautious, and are trying our best to not let these difficult times weigh us down.


Frankly, whether the positives outweigh the negatives or vice versa, is a rather difficult question to answer. However, I do believe that it is a learning curve. The quicker you understand and adapt, the easier things get.


Although one thing is for certain, for all of us who are mentally well-developed, will always remember what the old normal used to be like. The simpler days. Meanwhile, the children being born today will never truly know the difference.

Answer 04

Regarding the positives, I have had the time to slow down and truly focus on myself in ways that I had not before.


Strangely, I somehow also feel more secure in my relationships? It is as though we are all going through something terrible together. We find ways to socially interact with each other by forwarding memes and/or facetiming, all while not physically seeing each other.


That said, there was never a time where I felt lonely. I definitely think that the pandemic has generated many important topics for discussion, but I would not go as far as to say that the positives outweigh the negatives.


I wish it did not have to take hundreds of thousands of people to die for us to reflect on our actions as a society and to hold ourselves accountable.


Although, it is not all bad. It is a definite reality-check for many of us to realize first hand that we are not untouchable as a species. I believe we have a shot at working toward a more sustainable future if we all take something from this.

Answer 05

The extended amount of free time on my hands has allowed me to rethink and somewhat solve some personal (mental and physical) issues in my life as well as release some of the anxiety and stress I get from work and school, prior to quarantine.


In my case, quarantine was truly what my mental needed. The negatives are quite obvious in that we are all restricted from the outside world and all things public and social. Not to mention, boredom has taken a toll on us all and may continue doing so.


I wish it did not have to take hundreds of thousands of people to die for us to reflect on our actions as a society and to hold ourselves accountable.


Although, it is not all bad. It is a definite reality-check for many of us to realize first hand that we are not untouchable as a species. I believe we have a shot at working toward a more sustainable future if we all take something from this.

I feel privileged enough to say that quarantine has only been positive for me. I know many people have felt the negative effects and, although I understand them, luckily, I was able to find several blessings during these trying times.


For any first time investor, this would be the perfect time to capitalize and grow one’s wealth in platforms such as the stock market. Sadly, what might be some people’s misfortune, could be a blessing for others. I recognize this privilege.

Answer 07

Nowadays, I get to read, take time to cook up nice food, work out and go to sleep early. To me, the hardest part is being cut off from physical communication.


Not only do I find it difficult to stay in contact with friends but to try and manage providing emotional support for them through the phone has truly altered how I anticipate our interactions.

In regards to self-employed people like restaurant owners, it is significantly difficult to adapt to such changes.

Answer 16

Looking on the bright side of things, it is safe to say that, from my experience, the pandemic has brought families closer. Also, more time is given to work on projects and engage in activities, sports and hobbies.

Quarantine has affected me both in a positive and negative way. To start, some of the things I disliked the most was not being able to see my friends and family.

Not to mention, I started running out on things to do and it was hard for me to keep busy. I was bored, angry, upset and fed up at the fact that life was not "normal" anymore.


Moreover, school became frustrating as all courses switched to an online platform, which was quite difficult to overcome.


Going from seeing teachers and classmates in person, to strictly communicating with them through a screen was different, to say the least. A lot of things became unfair and it could not have been anything anyone was prepared for.


However, on the positive side, after a couple of months of being isolated, I started to embrace the idea of being a homebody and genuinely liked being around myself. I enjoyed my own company and started learning more about myself all while getting used to the whole quarantine situation. The latter also helped me build a stronger bond and connection to my parents which I am genuinely grateful for.

Quels ont été pour vous les impacts positifs ou négatifs de la quarantaine jusqu’à maintenant? Diriez-vous que les impacts positifs pèsent plus que ceux négatifs ou l’inverse?

Réponse 01

Positifs : apprentissage de nouveaux outils de télétravail; avantage de temps pour des contacts sociaux, même si ceux-ci étaient virtuels; économie d'argent, car il suffit d’avoir moins de consommation; avantage de temps pour des marches.


Négatifs: ne pas pouvoir être en direct contact avec mes enfants, mes proches, et ma clientèle; beaucoup d'inquiétude quant à la maladie elle-même; parfois il est difficile de suivre les consignes gouvernementales qui se contredisent souvent; être confiné, donc ne pas pouvoir me déplacer selon mes besoins; être sur mes gardes pour ne pas être contaminé et ne pas contaminer les autres; suivre les nouvelles qui n'abordent que ce sujet; beaucoup d'heures passées devant l'ordinateur pour le télétravail; mes yeux en ont souffert.

Réponse 02

Malheureusement, je pense que les impacts négatifs pèsent davantage. Ma vie scolaire/professionnelle est particulièrement touchée. Je m'ennuie énormément de mes proches. Je ne bouge pas autant qu'avant, donc ma santé est moins bonne. Je suis paradoxalement moins productive également. En revanche, j'ai amplement de sommeil et mon entourage est en santé.
